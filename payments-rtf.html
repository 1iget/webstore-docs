Payments: Regions, Fees, and Tiers

When you use Chrome Web Store Payments or created your digital goods through the Chrome Web Store interface, you need to choose from one of 18 price tiers for each region that you publish to. This page shows those price tiers for all regions that the store supports. It also gives details about who can use Chrome Web Store Payments, and how much of the purchase price you can expect to receive.

Transaction fees

When someone buys an item using Chrome Web Store Payments or the In-App Payments API, Google charges a 5% fee per transaction. For example, if you charge $1.99, you'll receive $1.89.

Availability

For developers/merchants

Developers in regions supported by Google Wallet can sign up for a merchant account to sell their Chrome App, Extension, Theme, hosted web app or virtual goods through in-app purchases.

For buyers (one time payments & Chrome Web Store managed in-app payments)

Note: some regions must include any applicable taxes in the the price charged to the user. Refer to the Developer Dashboard for more information.

For buyers (self managed in-app payments)

If you self manage your digital goods inventory using JWTs, you can sell your digital goods in any of the 140+ countries supported by Google Wallet. See the Buyer Availability section in regions supported by Google Wallet for more information.

Managing transactions, refunding money

You can see all transactions posted to your Google Wallet Merchant account by visiting the Google Wallet Merchant Center. Within the Merchant Center you can check the status of orders at a glance and respond to customer questions as quickly as possible. Check the documentation for further details about how to use the site.
